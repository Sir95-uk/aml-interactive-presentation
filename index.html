What is AMLAR?

AMLAR is the UK's comprehensive reform of Anti-Money Laundering regulations, replacing the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 (MLR 2017) with a modernized regulatory framework.

üéØ Key Changes Under AMLAR:

  • Supervision Reform: OPBAS will be abolished and replaced with a new FCA body that directly supervises professional enablers (accountants, lawyers, tax advisers)
  • Direct FCA Oversight: Professional body supervisors will no longer regulate AML compliance - FCA takes direct control
  • Crypto Asset Oversight: Comprehensive regulation of crypto asset businesses by FCA (since October 2023, with full conduct regulation coming 2024-2026)
  • Asset Recovery Powers: Enhanced powers for civil recovery and unexplained wealth orders
  • Risk-Based Approach: More flexible, proportionate requirements based on actual risk
  • Technology Integration: Recognition of digital identity verification and AI-powered monitoring

üîÑ MAJOR STRUCTURAL CHANGE - End of Professional Body Supervision:

Current System (Until AMLAR): Professional bodies (ICAEW, Law Society, etc.) supervise their members for AML, with OPBAS overseeing these bodies

New System (Under AMLAR): OPBAS abolished. FCA establishes new supervisory body to directly regulate professional enablers

Why this matters:

  • More consistent and rigorous supervision across the sector
  • Professional bodies can no longer "self-regulate" on AML
  • FCA will have direct enforcement powers over accountants, lawyers, and tax advisers
  • Stronger focus on high-risk professional enablers who facilitate money laundering
  • Better coordination with other FCA-supervised sectors

What is FATF?

The Financial Action Task Force is the global standard-setter for anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing (AML/CFT). FATF issues recommendations that countries must implement to combat financial crime effectively.

üö® 2027 Mutual Evaluation Review (MER)

Critical Date: The UK will undergo its next FATF Mutual Evaluation Review in 2027

What will be assessed:

  • Effectiveness of AMLAR implementation
  • Quality and usefulness of Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
  • Crypto asset regulation and supervision
  • Beneficial ownership transparency
  • International cooperation and information sharing
  • Asset recovery and confiscation measures

‚ö†Ô∏è Why the 2027 MER Matters:

A poor rating could lead to:

  • International reputational damage
  • Increased scrutiny of UK financial institutions
  • Higher compliance costs for cross-border transactions
  • Potential "grey listing" if deficiencies are severe

  • R.20-23: Suspicious transaction reporting
  • R.24-25: Beneficial ownership transparency
  • R.26-35: Supervision and enforcement
