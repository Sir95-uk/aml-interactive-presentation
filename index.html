Overview

AMLAR is a major UK reform programme aimed at strengthening the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regime, improving supervision, increasing corporate transparency and boosting asset-recovery powers.

Main Components

  • Revisions to the Money Laundering Regulations (MLRs)
  • Improved supervision across FCA, HMRC and PBSs via OPBAS
  • Companies House verification and transparency reforms
  • Crypto oversight expansion (Travel Rule, analytics)
  • Enhanced law enforcement powers under POCA
  • Asset recovery improvements (AFOs, crypto seizure powers)

MLR Enhancements

  • Expanded triggers for Enhanced Due Diligence
  • Clearer BO validation expectations
  • Stronger ongoing monitoring requirements
  • Crypto firms brought under stricter FCA oversight

Asset Recovery Improvements

  • Faster and broader Account Freezing Orders
  • Crypto seized from cold wallets
  • Improved cross-agency intelligence sharing
{/* FATF SECTION */}

Overview

FATF sets the global AML/CFT standards followed by over 200 jurisdictions. The UK aligns closely with FATF’s 40 Recommendations.

Key FATF Recommendations Relevant to UK Firms

  • Rec. 1 — risk assessments
  • Rec. 10 — CDD & ongoing monitoring
  • Rec. 12 — PEPs
  • Rec. 15 — cryptoasset regulation
  • Rec. 20 — SAR reporting quality
  • Rec. 24 — beneficial ownership transparency

UK Weaknesses Addressed by AMLAR

  • Inconsistent PBS supervision
  • Beneficial ownership data gaps
  • Crypto regulatory limitations
  • SAR system capacity issues
  • Slow asset recovery processes

Next FATF Mutual Evaluation Review — 2027

The UK’s next FATF MER will take place in 2027. FATF will closely examine the effectiveness of AMLAR reforms, crypto regulation, beneficial ownership transparency, SAR quality, and asset recovery performance.

What FATF Will Focus on in 2027

  • Supervisory effectiveness across FCA, HMRC & PBSs
  • Companies House identity verification enforcement
  • Crypto Travel Rule implementation
  • Use of AFOs and civil recovery powers
  • Quality and usefulness of SARs
  • High-risk sectors (legal, accountancy, TCSPs, crypto)
