üìã AMLAR Overview

Overview

AMLAR strengthens UK AML supervision, improves corporate transparency, and boosts asset recovery powers.

Key Areas:

  • Money Laundering Regulations (MLRs) revisions
  • FCA, HMRC, and OPBAS supervision improvements
  • Companies House verification reforms
  • Crypto oversight (Travel Rule, analytics)
  • Enhanced law enforcement and asset recovery powers

üìú Money Laundering Regulations

What Are They?

The Money Laundering Regulations set out requirements for businesses to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing.

Key Requirements:

  • Risk Assessment: Assess and document ML/TF risks
  • Customer Due Diligence: Verify customer identity
  • Enhanced Due Diligence: For high-risk customers
  • Record Keeping: Maintain records for 5 years
  • Suspicious Activity Reports: Report to NCA
  • Training: Regular AML training for staff

FCA (Financial Conduct Authority)

  • Supervises banks, payment institutions, e-money firms
  • Conducts thematic reviews and enforcement
  • Issues fines for non-compliance

HMRC (HM Revenue & Customs)

  • Supervises Money Service Businesses
  • High-value dealers, estate agents
  • Trust or company service providers

OPBAS

  • Oversees professional body supervisors
  • Accountants, legal professionals, tax advisers

üè¢ Companies House Reforms

What's Changing?

Strengthening verification to improve corporate transparency.

Key Reforms:

  • Identity Verification: Directors and PSCs must verify identity
  • Beneficial Ownership: Enhanced transparency
  • Filing Requirements: Stricter checks
  • Powers to Reject: Can reject suspicious filings
  • Information Sharing: Better data sharing with law enforcement

‚öñÔ∏è REP CRIM

Reporting Criminal Property

Requirement under POCA 2002 to report suspicions of money laundering.

Who Must Report?

  • All persons in regulated sector
  • Businesses supervised under MLRs

What to Report?

  • Knowledge or suspicion of money laundering
  • Criminal property

üìù How to Submit a SAR

Submit to National Crime Agency (NCA)

What to Include:

  • Subject details (name, address, DOB)
  • Description of suspicious activity
  • Reason for suspicion
  • Value and timing

‚úì Report as soon as practicable
‚úì Quality over quantity
‚úì Don't tip off the subject

üéØ Systems Prioritisation

Risk-Based Approach

Prioritise AML systems based on ML/TF risk level.

Means:

  • Identify highest risks
  • Allocate resources accordingly
  • Enhanced measures for high-risk
  • Simplified for low-risk (where appropriate)

High risk = Enhanced controls
Low risk = Standard controls
Never zero controls

üîç Risk Factors

Customer Risk

  • High: PEPs, complex structures
  • Medium: Standard business
  • Lower: Public companies, government

Geographic Risk

  • High: FATF high-risk jurisdictions
  • Lower: EEA, strong AML regimes

Product/Service Risk

  • High: Anonymous, cash, crypto
  • Lower: Face-to-face with audit trail

Channel Risk

  • High: Non-face-to-face
  • Lower: Face-to-face verification

‚öôÔ∏è Prioritising Controls

Step 1: Risk Assessment

Identify and assess ML/TF risks. Rate as High, Medium, or Low.

Step 2: Design Controls

Match control intensity to risk level. Enhanced for high-risk.

Step 3: Allocate Resources

Direct resources to highest-risk areas. More frequent monitoring for high-risk customers.

üõ°Ô∏è Enhanced Measures

Enhanced Due Diligence

  • Additional customer information
  • Source of funds and wealth
  • Senior management approval
  • Enhanced ongoing monitoring

Enhanced Monitoring

  • More frequent reviews
  • Lower alert thresholds
  • Manual review
  • Real-time for very high-risk

Document why high-risk and what measures applied
