Overview

AMLAR is a UK reform programme strengthening Anti-Money Laundering rules, improving supervision, corporate transparency, and asset recovery.

Main Components

  • Revisions to the Money Laundering Regulations (MLRs)
  • Improved supervision via FCA, HMRC, and OPBAS
  • Companies House verification and transparency reforms
  • Crypto oversight (Travel Rule, analytics)
  • Enhanced law enforcement powers under POCA
  • Asset recovery improvements (AFOs, crypto seizure powers)

MLR Enhancements

  • Expanded triggers for Enhanced Due Diligence
  • Clearer Beneficial Owner validation expectations
  • Stronger ongoing monitoring requirements
  • Crypto firms under stricter FCA oversight

Overview

FATF sets global AML/CFT standards for over 200 jurisdictions. The UK aligns closely with its 40 Recommendations.

Key Recommendations

  • Rec. 1 — Risk assessments
  • Rec. 10 — Customer Due Diligence & ongoing monitoring
  • Rec. 12 — Politically Exposed Persons
  • Rec. 15 — Cryptoasset regulation
  • Rec. 20 — SAR reporting quality
  • Rec. 24 — Beneficial ownership transparency

Next MER 2027

The UK’s next FATF Mutual Evaluation Review (MER) is scheduled for 2027. Focus areas will include:

  • Supervisory effectiveness across FCA, HMRC & PBSs
  • Companies House identity verification enforcement
  • Crypto Travel Rule implementation
  • Use of AFOs and civil recovery powers
  • SAR quality and asset recovery performance

REP CRIM

Under REP CRIM, authorized persons must report suspicions of money laundering. This applies to regulated firms under FSMA.

FSMA Updates

  • Changes affect authorisation requirements for firms
  • Updated supervision standards
  • Stronger enforcement for non-compliance
